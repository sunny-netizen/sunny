Street Networks

Street intersections of Europe extracted from OpenStreetMap.

OpenStreetMap is an open-source geographic database of the planet, maintained through a volunteer-driven collaboration.

It was a main data source for my masters dissertation, where I evaluated the correspondence between hierarchical structure in the road network of Europe and human geography.

I extracted the the road system of Europe from OpenStreetMap with the Python library OSMnx, modelled the road system as a network graph, implemented percolation analysis, constructed a hierarchical tree(dendrogram) from clusters, conducted phenetic analysis, and analyzed correlational statistics.

Creek to Ocean Plastics

Mapbox visualization of the Ballona Creek Watershed.

Ballona Creek is a channelized waterway running through southwestern Los Angeles County into the Pacific Ocean.

As a CivicSpark Fellow with the City of Culver City, I built capacity for climate resilience and sustainability efforts, including:

  • the Ballona Creek Revitalization Project
  • City Ban On Single-Use Plastics
  • Augmented Reality (AR) Stormwater Construction Educational Outreach

This experience inspired me to write a blog post I wrote on data and climate action in local government, including a Sankey Diagram.

My local involvement continued with a local nonprofit, Ballona Creek Renaissance. I created an Esri ArcGIS StoryMap for their outreach developed virtual drone footage for a watershed sustainability video they funded.

Planets

Number of exoplanets orbiting host stars.

Exoplanets or extrasolar planets are planets that orbit stars beyond our solar system.

Inspired by star maps and my own experience with observing exoplanets, I created a map of exoplanets using data from the NASA Exoplanet Archive. This map is intended for stargazers wishing to know which stars are orbited by confirmed exoplanets.

The NASA Exoplanet Archive helped me again to make a version of Clancy's Multiverse Simulator from the TV show, Midnight Gospel.
