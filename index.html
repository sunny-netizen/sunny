Street Networks

OpenStreetMap is an open-source geographic database of the planet, maintained through a volunteer-driven collaboration.

It was a main data source for my masters dissertation , where I revealed weak relationships between hierarchical structure in the road network of Europe and human geographies by:

  • Extracting the road system of Europe from OpenStreetMap as a network graph with Python library OSMnx .
  • Detecting hierarchical clusters with Percolation analysis , a form of unsupervised machine learning.
  • Constructing a dendrogram from the clusters and conducting phenetic analysis.

Creek to Ocean Plastics

Ballona Creek is a channelized waterway running through southwestern Los Angeles County into the Pacific Ocean.

Climate resilience and sustainability were my focus as a CivicSpark Fellow with the City of Culver City. I built capacity for:

  • Ballona Creek Revitalization Project
  • City Ban On Single-Use Plastics
  • Augmented Reality (AR) Stormwater Construction Educational Outreach

Following this experience I created a blog post on data and climate action in local government and a Sankey Diagram .

My local involvement continued with Ballona Creek Renaissance, an environmental nonprofit. I created an Esri ArcGIS StoryMap for their outreach developed virtual drone footage for a watershed sustainability video .

Planets

Exoplanets or extrasolar planets are planets that orbit stars beyond our solar system.

Inspired by star maps, I created a map of exoplanets using data from the NASA Exoplanet Archive. This map is intended for stargazers wishing to know which stars are orbited by confirmed exoplanets.

I used the same data source to create fictional worlds based on The Midnight Gospel.
